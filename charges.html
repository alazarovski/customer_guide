Part A. Type of Charges

The CRCO operates different charge recovery systems on behalf of both Member and non-Member States, in accordance with the guidelines recommended by the International Civil Aviation Organisation (ICAO).

A.1. Route Charges

The Member States of EUROCONTROL have adopted basic principles for a harmonised regional route charges system involving a single charge per flight. The CRCO operates this Multilateral Route Charges System with due regard to equity and transparency.

A.1.2. Calculation

A route charge is levied for each flight performed in the EUROCONTROL airspace. The airspace is divided into route charging zones as defined by the Member States.

The route charge calculation is based on three elements: \[\text{Charge} = \text{Distance Factor} \times \text{Aircraft Weight Factor} \times \text{Unit Rate of Charge}\]

The product of these three elements is the route charge for each charging zone. Refer to Annex A for a step-by-step example.

A.1.2.1. Distance Factor

The distance factor by charging zone is obtained by dividing the great circle distance (in kilometres) by \(100\): \[\text{Distance Factor} = \frac{\text{Great Circle Distance (km)}}{100}\]

  • Take-off and Landing Deduction: For each take-off and landing on the territory of a Member State, \(20\text{ km}\) are deducted from the total distance within the impacted charging zone(s).
  • Circular Flights: For flights terminating at the aerodrome of departure with no intermediate landing, the distance factor is determined by reference to the most distant point from the aerodrome.
Tip

Detailed distance factor calculation rules are outlined in Article 5 of the Conditions of Application of the Route Charges System, downloadable from the CRCO website.

A.1.2.2. Aircraft Weight Factor

The aircraft weight factor is calculated from the certificated maximum take-off weight (MTOW) of the aircraft, expressed in metric tonnes (to one decimal place): \[\text{Weight Factor} = \sqrt{\frac{\text{MTOW}}{50}}\]

  • Fleet Declarations: Operators must notify the CRCO of any change in their fleet’s composition (acquisitions, disposals, dry/wet leases) or MTOW updates before the last working day of the month using the Fleet Declaration module on CEFA.
  • Default MTOW Rule: In the event of no fleet declaration being received, the weight factor will be calculated using the MTOW of the heaviest aircraft of the same type known to exist.

A.1.2.3. Unit Rate of Charge

The unit rate of charge is the rate applied in a charging zone to a flight by a standard aircraft of \(50\text{ metric tonnes}\) (weight factor of \(1.00\)) over a distance factor of \(1.00\). * Unit rates are set on 1st January of each year. * To reduce exchange rate fluctuations, unit rates are adjusted monthly for States not using the Euro or not establishing their cost-base in Euro. Adjusted rates are published on the CRCO tariffs page.

Terminal charges bills are issued monthly in the middle of the month following the flights, alongside route charges. Payment terms are the same (30 days from the date of the bill).

A.2.2. Calculation

Terminal charges are levied on departing flights only, within designated terminal charging zones (aerodromes or groups of aerodromes): \[\text{Terminal Charge} = \text{Aircraft Weight Factor} \times \text{Unit Rate of Charge for Aerodrome}\]

A.2.2.1. Aircraft Weight Factor

Depending on the State, two different formulas are used to calculate the terminal weight factor:

  • Formula 1 (Standard EU)
  • Formula 2 (Linear)

Applied by: Albania, Bulgaria, Croatia, Denmark, Finland, France, Greece, Hungary, Ireland, Italy, Lithuania, Malta, The Netherlands, Slovenia, Spain, and Sweden: \[\text{Weight Factor} = \left(\frac{\text{MTOW}}{50}\right)^{0.70}\]

Applied by: Moldova and North Macedonia: \[\text{Weight Factor} = \text{MTOW (in metric tonnes)}\]

The MTOW value used is identical to that used for route charges (see Section A.1.2.2).

A.2.2.2. Unit Rate of Charge

Unit rates are applied in EUR, except for Denmark (DKK) and Sweden (SEK). Rates can vary by airport. Airport-specific rates are listed in the latest Information Circulars under the “Key Documents” section on the CRCO portal.

A.3. Air Navigation Charges

Air navigation charges are billed on behalf of non-Member States of EUROCONTROL. They can cover route charges, terminal charges, or both.

A.3.1. Participating States

The CRCO is entrusted with billing and collection for the following States:

State Route Charges Terminal Charges Invoice Currency
Egypt Yes Yes EUR
Morocco Yes No EUR
Uzbekistan Yes No EUR

A.3.2. Calculation of Route Charges

The calculation elements are identical to the Multilateral System: \[\text{Route Charge} = \text{Distance Factor} \times \text{Aircraft Weight Factor} \times \text{Unit Rate of Charge}\]

  • Distance Factor: Great circle distance in km divided by \(100\). For each take-off and landing within the State, \(20\text{ km}\) are deducted.
  • Weight Factor: Standard formula: \(\sqrt{\text{MTOW}/50}\).
  • Unit Rate: Set at the beginning of the year and remains valid for the calendar year.

A.3.3. Calculation of Terminal Charges (Egypt)

Terminal charges are levied on departing flights only:

A.3.3.1. Aircraft Weight Factor (Egypt)

Egypt calculates the terminal weight factor using: \[\text{Weight Factor} = \left(\frac{\text{MTOW}}{50}\right)^{0.50}\]

A.3.3.2. Unit Rate of Charge

Rates can differ by airport and are published in the CRCO Information Circulars.

A.4. Communication Charges

The CRCO bills and collects communication charges in the Shanwick Oceanic Region on behalf of AIRNAV IRELAND for services provided by the Shanwick Aeronautical Radio Station.

  • Calculation: Flat rate (in EUR) per flight.
  • Billing & Terms: Invoiced monthly in the middle of the following month, with standard 30-day payment terms.
